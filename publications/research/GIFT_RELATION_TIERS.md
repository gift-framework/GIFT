## 1. Classification Criteria

### 1.1 Tier 1: Arithmetic Pure

**Definition**: Relations that are mathematical identities involving only:
- Integer arithmetic (+, -, ×, ÷)
- GIFT integer constants
- No transcendental numbers
- No experimental comparison

**Falsifiability**: None (these are tautologies once GIFT constants are fixed)

**Example**:
$$196883 = 47 \times 59 \times 71 = L_8 \times (b_3 - L_6) \times (b_3 - 6)$$

### 1.2 Tier 2: φ-Powered

**Definition**: Relations involving the golden ratio φ:
- Include φ or ln(φ) or φ^n
- Connect GIFT constants to transcendental structure
- Not directly experimentally verifiable (φ is irrational)

**Falsifiability**: Would be falsified if φ were shown NOT to emerge from E₈/G₂/K₇

**Example**:
$$\frac{m_\mu}{m_e} = 27^\phi \quad (\phi = \frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2})$$

### 1.3 Tier 3: Physical Observable

**Definition**: Relations that predict experimental values:
- Dimensionless ratios matching measured quantities
- Direct comparison to PDG/Planck/etc. data
- Include deviation percentages

**Falsifiability**: Direct experimental test

**Example**:
$$\sin^2\theta_W = \frac{b_2}{b_3 + \dim_{G_2}} = \frac{21}{91} = 0.2308 \quad (\text{Exp: } 0.2312, \text{Dev: } 0.17\%)$$
