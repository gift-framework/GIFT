1. Foundational Architecture

The GIFT framework is built upon a specific chain of dimensional reduction, E₈×E₈ → K₇ → Standard Model, where each component is chosen for its unique mathematical properties.

1.1 Core Structures

The product structure E₈×E₈ (dimension 496) is required for anomaly cancellation in heterotic string theory and provides sufficient degrees of freedom for the Standard Model and a potential hidden sector.
* Internal Manifold (K₇): A compact seven-dimensional manifold with G₂ holonomy is used for dimensional reduction. This choice preserves the minimal N=1 supersymmetry in four dimensions, which is consistent with the existence of chiral fermions.
* Cohomology and Physics: Physical fields are mapped to the manifold's cohomology. The Betti numbers determine the multiplicity of fields:
  * Gauge Fields: The second Betti number, b₂(K₇) = 21, corresponds to the number of harmonic 2-forms, providing the basis for gauge fields.
  * Matter Fields: The third Betti number, b₃(K₇) = 77, corresponds to harmonic 3-forms, providing the basis for matter fields. The framework posits that this naturally accommodates three generations of fermions.

1.2 The Torsion Principle

Physical interactions are sourced by a controlled deviation from perfect G₂ holonomy. When the defining 3-form φ is not perfectly closed (dφ ≠ 0), the manifold develops torsion.

* Torsion as Interaction: The magnitude of this torsion, κ_T, is not arbitrary but is itself derived from topological invariants.
* Geometry as RG Flow: The geodesic flow on this torsionful manifold is identified with the Renormalization Group (RG) evolution of physical couplings, with the affine parameter λ corresponding to the logarithmic energy scale ln(μ).
