# Conjecture CF: finite closed-form compact torsion-free metric

## Status

Separate research programme. Not implied by Level E.

## Conjectural target

Determine whether there exists a finite collection of explicit forms `Theta_1, ..., Theta_M` and explicit coefficients `c_j(R)` in an allowed closed-form field such that

`phi_R = sum_{j=1}^M c_j(R) Theta_j`

and both equations hold exactly:

- `d phi_R = 0`,
- `d (*_{phi_R} phi_R) = 0`.

## Three branches

### CF-A

Show that the closed small-torsion family already satisfies

`d (*_{widetilde phi_R} widetilde phi_R) = 0`

without further correction.

### CF-B

Find a finite-dimensional ansatz class stable under:

- Donaldson reconstruction,
- collar-to-bulk matching,
- Hodge-star nonlinearity,
- anisotropic perturbation.

### CF-C

If no finite invariant ansatz closes, state the honest endpoint:

explicit datum plus certified analytic existence plus computable convergent correction.

## Rule of language

Do not use the phrase "closed-form metric on `K_7`" unless the final compact expression is finite and checked directly against both torsion equations.
